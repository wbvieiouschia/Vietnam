SHOPPING TOURISM

Shopping tourism in Vietnam offers travelers the chance to purchase unique local products, handicrafts, fashion, and souvenirs while exploring the country’s markets and commercial districts.

1. Traditional Markets

Experience Vietnam’s vibrant market culture where locals sell food, crafts, and everyday goods.

Famous Local Markets Local Products & Souvenirs
Ben Thanh Market in Ho Chi Minh City and Dong Xuan Market in Hanoi are bustling hubs for fresh produce, handicrafts, and everyday items. Tourists can buy clothing, handicrafts, spices, and local food while interacting with friendly vendors, experiencing authentic Vietnamese culture.

2. Handicraft and Artisan Products

Explore traditional Vietnamese craftsmanship and locally made artisan products.

Traditional Crafts Craft Villages
Vietnam is renowned for handmade items such as silk, ceramics, bamboo products, embroidery, and lacquerware crafted with traditional techniques. Bat Trang (pottery) and Van Phuc (silk weaving) offer hands-on experiences and authentic locally produced goods for visitors.

3. Modern Shopping Centers

For a contemporary shopping experience, Vietnam’s cities provide modern malls and department stores.

Malls & Department Stores Comfortable Shopping Experience
Major cities feature malls with international brands, fashion boutiques, electronics, and luxury goods. Air-conditioned spaces, restaurants, and entertainment zones make shopping convenient and enjoyable for tourists.

4. Night Markets and Street Shopping

Evening markets offer a lively, social atmosphere for browsing and tasting local specialties.

Night Market Culture Bargaining Experience
Markets come alive at night, offering souvenirs, clothing, accessories, and street food for both locals and tourists. Bargaining is expected, making shopping interactive, fun, and culturally immersive.

Shopping tourism allows visitors to take home unique Vietnamese products while supporting local artisans, craft villages, and small businesses.
